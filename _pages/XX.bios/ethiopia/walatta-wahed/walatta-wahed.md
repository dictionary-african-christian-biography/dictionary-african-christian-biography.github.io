W&auml;l&auml;tt&auml;-Wahed (perhaps fl. late 13th - early 14th century A.D.), was the baptismal name given by St. Täklä-Haymanot to the wife of Käfär-Wedem, the governor of Adamo, whom the saint baptized together with his family.

Richard K. P. Pankhurst
